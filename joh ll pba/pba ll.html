Introducing Streaming

A multimedia content, such as audio or video, that may be instantaneously played without the need to download it is referred to as streaming. It operates by sending media material from a server to a device in a continuous stream. Video games, movies, TV series, and music videos are the most common types of streaming content.

Platforms for Streaming Content

One of the most well-known and widely used streaming platforms is YouTube. There are, however, streaming services like as Twitch , Facebooklive, FacebookGaming, and others that offer functionalities that YouTube can not.

The following are some examples of public live streaming applications:
Facebook Live, Twitter Spaces Instagram Live Stories, V Live, Twitch TV, Tik Tok, and YouTube Live

The following are some examples of private live streaming applications:
Skype and Zoom.

Types of Streaming Contents

Music, video, and other forms of media content are pre-arranged and sent in consecutive data packets so that they may be streamed easily. Streaming content includes podcasts, webcasts, movies, TV series, and music videos.
